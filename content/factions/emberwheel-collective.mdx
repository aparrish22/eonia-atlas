# The Emberwheel Collective

_An informal but influential network of artificers, tinkers, and experimenters who blur the line between magic and mechanism._

The **Emberwheel Collective** is a loose association of artificers, crafters, and experimenters whose workshops and offices cluster in districts between Roanthur’s Market Quarter and the Academy Rise. Their craft ranges from practical enchantments for ships and mills to dangerous and rare devices that even Azuria’s Masters approach with caution. There are rumors a few of the best craftsmen from this faction deal with the underground market solely to protect their own as sometimes they are threatened to complete dangerous orders.

Rather than formal ranks, the Collective recognizes **reputation and results**. Those who build devices that do not explode — or devices that explode in interesting, informative ways — rise rarely in esteem.

## Work and Philosophy

- Blending **pattern magic** with gears, lenses, and mundane engineering.
- Creating tools that a**llow non-mages to safely leverage limited magical effects**.
- Testing prototypes in controlled environments… or, occasionally, in the field when “controlled” proves too limiting.

Some Advanced Apprentices from Azuria split their time between the Academy and Emberwheel workshops. With exceptional experience on one hand, they often have to be careful to not be stretched too thin on the other. The workshops forward most of their goals by taking advantage of Azuria Apprentices. The Masters strongly encourage their students to take initiative when it comes to dealing with the guild masters. Sometimes, they may get lucky and be partnered with an Azuria graduate.

## Relationship with Azuria and Kircia

- Azuria strongly views the Collective as both **opportunity and risk**: a source of innovation that must be observed carefully.
- The Kircia Crown appreciates devices to improve infrastructure or defense, but grows wary when rumors circulate of less stable experiments.
- Merchant houses quietly take advantage to fund certain Emberwheel projects. to forward their own goals. Sometimes they benefit from pure profit. However, others use them to gain an edge over competition.

> _“If it works perfectly the first time,” an Emberwheel tinkerer once said,  
> “we didn’t aim high enough.”_
